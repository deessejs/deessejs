# Commercial Next.js SaaS template market — 2026-06-26 deep research

Triggered by user question on "how to properly sell" the template. Deep-research workflow ran 5 angles × 97 agent calls, verified 25 claims (16 confirmed, 9 refuted). Cites are primary vendor pricing pages verified on 2026-06-26.

## Verified 2026 price benchmarks (one-time, lifetime)

| Vendor | Entry | Mid | Top |
|---|---|---|---|
| Makerkit | $299 (was $349) | $599 (was $649) | — |
| ShipFast | $199 (was $299) | $249 (was $349) | $299 bundle (was $648) |
| TurboStarter | $299 (was $399) | $449 (was $549) | — |
| supastarter | €349 solo | €799 startup | €1,499 agency |
| SaasRock | $249 MVP | $499 Core | $1,999 Pro |
| Nexty | $188 (was $256) | — | Enterprise custom |

**Median:** $249-$299 entry / $499-$649 mid / $999-$1,499 top. **Only 2 vendors cross $1,000** (supastarter Agency, SaasRock Pro).

## 8 confirmed findings

1. **Tier count: 2-3 paid is canonical** — no competitor has more than 3 paid tiers
2. **100% of competitors sell lifetime licenses** — no subscription option anywhere; nobody defines "lifetime" except SaasRock ("Free updates for 1.x")
3. **Feature gating is structural** (seats / project scope / support tier), not capability-based — even the cheapest paid tier ships the full SaaS stack
4. **Free/Open Source tier is optional** — Makerkit + Nexty have one (lead magnet), supastarter + ShipFast + TurboStarter + SaasRock don't, all still sell
5. **Founder pricing: cohort caps > time deadlines** — ShipFast's "$100 off, 8350 customers, 12 remaining" live counter is the dominant pattern; strike-through framing is common; no countdown timers observed
6. **Agency/white-label lives in the top tier**, not as add-on — supastarter Agency €1,499 adds 10 seats + client licensing + private Discord + onboarding; ShipFast has no agency tier at all
7. **Enterprise tier is aspirational, not transactional** — Nexty is the only one with a "Contact Us" placeholder; zero competitors publish SLA/escrow/training/pricing floors. Enterprise for templates is a contact form, not a revenue line.
8. **Revenue benchmark: ShipFast 8338 paying customers** — implies $1.5M-$2.5M cumulative gross on entry tier alone. Realistic 12-month target for well-shipped template: $200k-$800k cumulative gross.

## What was NOT verified (still open)

- Free→paid and paid→higher-tier conversion rates
- Channel conversion rates for the template category (PH, Twitter/X, Reddit, HN, YouTube, SEO/GEO)
- Whether supastarter €1,499 / SaasRock $1,999 are real revenue or aspirational
- How long cohort caps take to fill

## Anti-pattern observed

SaasRock's $1,999 Pro vs $499 Core: feature delta is just "All RockStacks Latest" vs "RockStack Latest" — a $1,500 premium that is weakly justified. Consistent with the broader risk that $1,000+ tiers in this category have thin value narratives.

## Implication for DeesseJS

Current pricing (Starter $249→$399, Pro $499→$599, Team $899→$999, Lite free) is in the median band on every tier — no price change needed. Specific recommendations:
- Drop the $99 founding-member tier OR rebrand as single-tier "Lifetime deal — Founding Member" (4 price points for 3 structural tiers is a decision-matrix problem)
- Replace July 31 deadline with ShipFast-style live cohort counter ("73 of 250 slots remaining")
- Scope "Lifetime updates" to "DeesseJS 2.x" (SaasRock pattern)
- Reduce capability gating — move admin/blog/i18n to Starter; differentiate on seats/scope/support tier
- Drop the "Enterprise" implication from copy — Cloud + Team covers the high end
